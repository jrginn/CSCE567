Field Goal Visualizations

Introduction

In football, a field goal is a play where the team possessing the ball attempts to kick it through the uprights on the other teams’ side of the field. If they succeed, they gain three points. Otherwise, the other team gains possession where the kicking team kicked the ball from. These plays are typically executed on 4th down or at the expiration of the half. Many factors go into a team’s decision to kick a field goal or attempt a different play: the kicker’s skill, the weather, the score, the offense’s ability to gain yards, and more. In recent years, the NFL has made more data publicly available. Detailed play-by-play data now exists for every game played in the NFL each year. The data for this project was sourced here for the 2024 season. Cleaning was performed on the data to make it more relevant and fix some quality issues.

Field Goal Accuracy and Distance

The visualization below shows how accuracy of field goals change as the kicks get farther away. By hovering over a bar, you can see the distance kicked from, the number of kicks attempted from this distance, and the percentage of those kicks made. The color of the bar is darker where the percentage made is higher. You can also filter stadium type (closed or open/no roof), wind speed, temperature, team, and week the kick was attempted. Weeks 1-18 are in the regular season, while 19-22 are in the playoffs. The percentage of kicks made decreases as distance increases.

Field Goal Percentage

This pie chart shows the percentage of field goals made, blocked, or missed. Hover over a section of the pie chart to see the number of kicks in that category. This is also filterable. The postseason actually has a higher percentage of kicks made, but this could be due to a smaller sample size.

Kick Distance versus Field Goal Probability

Field goal probability is an advanced statistic included with the nflverse play-by-play data. This gives the approximate likelihood of a field goal being completed when it is kicked. As the distance of the kick increases, the probability goes down. By hovering over a data point, you can see who kicked it and for what team, what the result of the kick was, what stadium the kick happened at, the temperature and wind, and the current score differential. Similar filters exist from the other visualizations.

Probability Heatmap

This shows the average field goal probability across five yard ranges for different NFL teams. The trend of longer distances decreasing probability is visible here. The color is darker where teams are more likely to make field goals, and this allows you to see which teams struggle more or less compared to others. Regions that are white are where that team has not kicked from.

Kicker Salary and Accuracy

This bar chart explores how individual kickers perform compared to how much they were paid in 2024. The darker the bar, the more accurate the kicker was. The highest paid kickers were not always the most accurate. NFL contracts are not negotiated every year, so many kickers are paid according to their performance as of a few years ago. This is why some very accurate kickers are around the middle of the payscale, while some more highly paid kickers struggled in 2024.
